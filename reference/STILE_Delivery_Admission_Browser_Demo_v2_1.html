Structural Integration Leverage

STILE separates structural delivery admission from transport observation and consumption observation. Transport can move data. Consumption can record use. Neither becomes the sole authority over the bounded structural admission decision.

Resolutionstructural_delivery_state = resolve(declared_delivery_structure, versioned_rules)
Transporttransport establishes movement
Structurestructure establishes admissibility
Consumptionconsumption establishes use

Three independent lanes

A transport event can change without rewriting the structural decision. A consumption event can change later without rewriting either the declared delivery structure or its admission result.

Lane 1

Structural Admission

Resolves the declared sender, receiver, message identity, payload identity, intent, expectation, context, and conflict state.

Current stateADMITTED
Lane 2

Transport Observation

Records an operational observation such as not observed, sent, received, or failed. It does not rewrite the structural admission decision.

Current stateNOT_OBSERVED
Lane 3

Consumption Observation

Records whether the admitted content is unread, consumed, rejected, or still unknown. It remains separate from delivery admission.

Current stateUNKNOWN

What each hash means

The evidence model separates the identity of declared structure, the structural decision, and later observations.

Structure Hash

Identifies the declared structural core under the named profile and schema. Transport and consumption observations are outside this hash.

Decision Hash

Identifies the structural resolution, delivery admission, reason codes, and the structure hash that produced them.

Observation Hash

Identifies transport and consumption observations bound to the structural decision without rewriting that decision.

Conformance
